\subsection*{Stokes-Einstein Equation}
\begin{equation*}
D = \frac{k_B T}{6 \pi \eta r}
\end{equation*}
where $k_B = 1.38 \times 10^{-23}$~J/K, $T = 294.15$~K, $\eta = 0.00098$~Pa$\cdot$s, $r$ = bead radius.

%==============================================================================
\subsection*{Summary}
%==============================================================================

Sessions 1--2 established the microscopy workflow: K\"ohler illumination alignment, camera calibration using a stage micrometer, sample preparation with thick chambers (Parafilm spacers), and Brownian motion video capture. A critical calibration error was identified and corrected in Session 2 (pixel size changed from 68.45~nm to 345~nm per pixel).

Particle tracking using ImageJ MTrack2 and Python analysis successfully produced:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Trajectory plots showing random walk behavior
    \item Displacement histograms fitted to Gaussian distributions
    \item Mean Squared Displacement (MSD) plots with linear fits
\end{itemize}

However, the measured diffusion coefficients are significantly higher than theoretical predictions (20--40$\times$), suggesting systematic issues that require further investigation.
